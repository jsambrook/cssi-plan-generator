# Seed: Local Bakery Same-Day Delivery Network

## Idea as stated

A coordination service that lets small independent bakeries offer same-day delivery without hiring their own drivers or paying high platform fees.

## Clarifying questions and answers

**What problem does this solve?**
Small bakeries lose sales to supermarkets and large chains that offer delivery. Platforms like DoorDash and UberEats charge 15-30% commissions, which wipes out margins on low-ticket items like bread and pastries. Most independent bakeries have no delivery capability and can't afford to build one.

**Who is the customer?**
Independent bakeries in mid-size cities (population 100k-500k). Not high-end patisseries (they can absorb delivery costs) and not rural bakeries (too spread out). The sweet spot: neighborhood bakeries doing $300k-$1M in annual revenue that are losing foot traffic to convenience-oriented competitors.

**What is the rough mechanism?**
Coordinate shared delivery routes across multiple bakeries in a neighborhood. A customer orders from any participating bakery by 10am, and all orders in a zone are batched and delivered in a single afternoon run. The driver visits 3-5 bakeries, picks up all orders, and delivers within a 3-mile radius. The bakeries split the delivery cost, making it affordable per order.

**What makes this hard to copy?**
Route density is the moat — you need enough bakeries in a zone to make shared routes work. Once you have 5-8 bakeries in a zone, a competitor starting from zero can't offer the same unit economics. But this is also the chicken-and-egg problem: you need bakeries to get density, and you need density to attract bakeries.

## Research findings

- The average independent bakery operates on 5-8% net margins (Source: IBISWorld Bakery Industry Report).
- DoorDash and UberEats charge bakeries 15-30% commission; many bakeries report delivery orders are unprofitable after platform fees (Source: Restaurant Business Online, multiple Reddit threads from bakery owners).
- Milk-run delivery models (batched route, multiple pickups) reduce per-stop cost by 40-60% compared to point-to-point delivery (Source: McKinsey last-mile delivery analysis).
- There are ~33,000 retail bakeries in the US, with the highest density in metros and college towns (Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics).

## Shaped seed

A shared delivery coordination service for independent bakeries. The mechanism: batch orders across 5-8 bakeries in a zone, run one afternoon milk-run route, split delivery costs. Target unit economics: $2-3 per order delivery cost (vs. $5-8 for individual delivery or 15-30% platform fees on a $12 average order).

Target market: independent bakeries in mid-size US cities doing $300k-$1M revenue, losing sales to delivery-enabled competitors. Beachhead: one zone in one city with 5-8 bakeries within a 3-mile radius.

Critical risk: the chicken-and-egg problem. You need zone density to make the economics work, but you need the economics to recruit bakeries. The first zone must be hand-built through direct relationships, then used as proof for the next zone.
