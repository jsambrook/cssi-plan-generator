# TOC Tension Resolution

This is the process for surfacing and resolving a core conflict inside a business idea. It is a simplified version of the Evaporating Cloud technique from Theory of Constraints, adapted for single-idea refinement.

Use this when the idea contains a buried "we need X but we also need not-X" that is preventing progress. See `references/mutate.md` Move 5 for when to trigger this.

---

## Step 1: Name the tension

Start by stating the tension as plainly as possible. Look for sentences in the idea that feel contradictory:

- "We need to be cheap enough to spread virally, but premium enough to fund development."
- "We need to automate the hard parts, but the hard parts require human judgment."
- "We need to target a broad market, but our mechanism only works for a narrow use case."

If you cannot find a tension, look for the thing that makes the idea feel "too good to be true" - there is usually a conflict hiding there.

---

## Step 2: Frame the Evaporating Cloud

The cloud has five elements:

| Element | Label | Question |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Common objective | O | What are both sides trying to achieve? |
| Need A | NA | What does side A need in order to achieve O? |
| Need B | NB | What does side B need in order to achieve O? |
| Action A | AA | What action satisfies NA but conflicts with NB? |
| Action B | AB | What action satisfies NB but conflicts with AA? |

Write these out explicitly. The tension is between AA and AB - you cannot do both.

**Example:**

- O: Build a business that grows and is financially sustainable
- NA: Charge enough to cover costs and generate margin
- NB: Spread widely to build network effects
- AA: Price at $200/month
- AB: Make it free

The conflict is AA vs. AB: you cannot simultaneously charge $200/month and be free.

---

## Step 3: Surface the assumptions

For each arrow in the cloud, ask: "What assumption makes this arrow feel necessary?"

- Why does achieving O require NA? (assumption behind the O-NA arrow)
- Why does achieving O require NB? (assumption behind the O-NB arrow)
- Why does NA require AA? (assumption behind the NA-AA arrow)
- Why does NB require AB? (assumption behind the NB-AB arrow)
- Why does AA conflict with AB? (assumption behind the conflict arrow)

Write one assumption per arrow. These are usually implicit beliefs that feel obvious until you say them out loud.

---

## Step 4: Challenge the assumptions

Look at each assumption and ask: "Is this always true? Under what conditions would it be false?"

The goal is to find one assumption that can be invalidated or weakened. When you find it, you have a potential injection.

**Example:** The assumption behind NB-AB ("we need to be free to spread widely") can be challenged: "What if the product spreads through outputs rather than through price? If every proposal generated by the tool is watermarked with a tasteful attribution, it spreads without being free."

---

## Step 5: State the injection

An injection is a concrete change to the idea that breaks the conflict by invalidating the challenged assumption.

Write the injection as a single sentence: "Instead of [old approach], we [new approach], which means we no longer need to assume [assumption]."

Then rewrite the relevant part of the idea to incorporate the injection.

---

## Output

After completing this process, the variant file should include:
- The named tension (one sentence)
- The five-element cloud (brief, table format is fine)
- The challenged assumption (one sentence)
- The injection (one sentence)
- The revised idea section that incorporates the injection
