# Sales Process Engineering (Justin Roff-Marsh / Ballistix)

Source: Justin Roff-Marsh, *The Machine: A Radical Approach to the Design of the Sales Function* (2015) and *Reengineering the Sales Process* (2006). Ballistix has applied SPE across scores of companies and industries for 20+ years.

---

## Core Thesis

Traditional sales organizations treat salespeople as independent agents responsible for the entire sales cycle — prospecting, qualifying, presenting, quoting, closing, and account management. This is the equivalent of pre-industrial craft production applied to sales. It wastes the most expensive resource (the salesperson's selling time) on low-value activities.

Sales Process Engineering applies manufacturing principles — division of labor, centralized scheduling, and drum-buffer-rope synchronization — to the sales function.

---

## Key Principles

### 1. Division of Labor

Separate sales activities by type and assign them to the right people:

- **Salespeople** do only one thing: conduct business-development conversations (appointments). Target: 5 per day, 5 days per week.
- **Sales support team** handles scheduling, CRM updates, follow-up, quoting, and administrative work.
- **Engineers/technical staff** handle field visits, solution design, and technical validation.
- **Operations** owns revenue responsibility and account management for existing customers.

### 2. Centralized Scheduling

A central team schedules all sales activities. Salespeople do not self-schedule, prospect, or manage their own pipeline. Work is pushed to salespeople the way a production scheduler pushes work to machines — maintaining 60-90 opportunities in the queue at all times.

### 3. Operations Owns Revenue

Revenue is the responsibility of Operations, not Sales. Sales focuses exclusively on the pursuit of *new* business. Existing customer relationships, renewals, expansions, and service are handled by the operations team that delivers the work.

### 4. Inside Sales as Default

Sales is primarily an inside activity. Field visits are the exception, performed by engineers or technical staff when face-to-face is genuinely required. Regional sales offices become redundant.

### 5. Fixed Compensation

Salespeople earn fixed salaries at market rate. Commissions and piece-rate pay are eliminated. The rationale: if the organization controls what salespeople do (centralized scheduling, defined activities), then individual incentive compensation is both unnecessary and counterproductive.

### 6. Eliminate the SDR Role

Roff-Marsh considers Sales Development Representatives a sub-optimization. SDRs may increase a salesperson's win rate, but they reduce total volume of business won by inserting junior, inexperienced people into the prospect's first interaction. Prospects deserve to talk to experienced professionals from the start.

### 7. Speed-Based Operating System

Roff-Marsh opposes the pursuit of efficiency as the primary decision-making framework. Instead, optimize for *speed* — how fast opportunities move through the pipeline. A healthy business has two value chains (business-as-usual and growth), both optimized for throughput speed. See his mini-book *An Ode to Speed*.

## TOC Connections

SPE is explicitly built on Theory of Constraints:

- **Constraint identification**: The constraint in most sales organizations is the salesperson's available selling time. Everything else (prospecting, admin, quoting) is protective capacity that should be offloaded.
- **Drum-Buffer-Rope**: The sales process is synchronized using DBR principles — the constraint (selling conversations) sets the pace; buffers protect it from starvation; the rope controls the release of new opportunities into the system.
- **Throughput focus**: SPE measures throughput (opportunities processed, deals closed) rather than efficiency metrics (calls per day, cost per lead).

---

## The "Machine" Metaphor

The sales function should operate like a production line:

1. Marketing generates raw material (leads)
2. Support team qualifies and schedules (work preparation)
3. Salespeople execute conversations (the constraint operation)
4. Engineers provide technical support (subordinate to the constraint)
5. Operations delivers and retains (downstream flow)

Each role is specialized. No one multitasks across roles. The system is managed as a whole, not as a collection of individuals.

---

## Relevance to TOC Consulting Practice

SPE is directly applicable when:

- A client's sales team is the constraint (or near-constraint)
- Salespeople are spending >50% of time on non-selling activities
- The client perceives "we need more salespeople" when the real issue is utilization
- Account management is consuming selling capacity
- Sales forecasting is unreliable because the process is unmanaged

SPE provides a concrete, implementable injection for the common cloud: "We need salespeople to own relationships AND we need salespeople to maximize new business development."
